Esplanade Website Redesign

Concept · UI/UX Design
Description
To improve the user experience of Esplanade website by redesigning the Information Architecture and key pages layout.
Skiils
Content Inventory, User Interviews, Competitive Analysis, Tree testing, Card sorting, Modular Design, Prototyping, Usability testing
Time Frame
2 Weeks
Role
Product Designer
Tools
Axure RP, Treejack, Sketch, Post-it,

Esplanade is a not-for-profit arts centre, socially motivated by the mission to entertain, engage, educate and inspire communities through the arts. Other than performing venues, the Esplanade also contains retail and meeting venues, as well as other lifestyle and arts related services which contribute a significant percentage to their total annual revenue (40%).

Research

What are the contents the website contains?

We started the project by conducting content inventory to get an overview of the existing contents on the website.

User research & Interviews

Derived from business annual report for Esplanade and the content inventory we came up with 4 main scenarios and tasks. We conducted interviews and usability testing on the existing site to see how users navigate to find the tasks and scenarios given. We wanted to understand what are the reasons and objectives of people who use the site? And we also wanted evaluate the heuristics of the website before coming up with a problem statement and hypothesize.

Issue Prioritisation
  1. Confusing Double Navigation Bar & Too Many Menu Items

    5/6 users had difficulty finding the ‘Visitor Guide’
    3/6 users had difficulty locating the ‘Eat & Drink’ menu item 3/6 users were unsure about the difference between 'What's On' and 'Explore'

  2. Cluttered Content

    3/6 users could not figure out which MRT station was closest as they were distracted by the map or did not know to scroll further down for the info

  3. Insufficient Information

    4/6 users were frustrated at having to click on each individual venue page to access basic venue information such as seating capacity

  4. Unclear CTA

    3/6 users did not notice the online venue request form

  5. Inflexible Filters and Too Many Results

    6/6 users manually scrolled through multiple pages to find an appropriate event
