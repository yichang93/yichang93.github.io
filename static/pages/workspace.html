Workspace


UI/UX Design
Period
2020 Nov - Present
Role
UX Designer/Product Owner
Skills
User Research, Copy Writing, Design System
Overview
Screening Eagle, formerly called Proceq, was specialized in NDT portable sensor business. Recently, the company has slowly transitioned its business into the SaaS model. And there are tons of user-generated data stored on its cloud. The company identified an opportunity to build a cloud-based web platform that makes these data more accessible to the users and links all the products together to create a powerful ecosystem. More information about the Workspace

Discovery

Stakeholder Interviews

The Workspace project is a very ambitious project that has a broad vision. Hence, the first step I took was to conduct a few rounds of stakeholder interviews to understand the short-term and long-term goals and the business considerations behind the decisions.

Internal user & product experts

The stakeholder interviews helped me understand that the Workspace is going to be a platform that allows the individual product teams to provide services to their users. And as the first step, we should make users' data available and accessible on Workspace. So it's crucial to understand the different needs of different products and their users. Interviewing the internal employees, e.g., the regional sales and the sensors' product managers, helped me understand the customer segments and the products.

Research & Findings

User interviews

After gathering the initial requirements, I noticed a few knowledge gaps in my team. We decided to talk to the users in person to narrow the gap. We derived the questions from the 3 main questions:

  • What does the data process flow of our user look like?
  • What are the data that are more important to our users?
  • How do our users feel about the data viewers in our existing products?
On-site Workers & Engineers

From the discussion with the internal employees, we learned there are, in general, 2 types of users: on-site workers and civil engineers.

During the recruitment, we tried to have a good mix of both types. And we found 2 on-site workers, 2 civil engineers and 1 doing both for our user interviews.

Findings
  • The users want to see measurement data and probe information.
  • The context of the measurements is crucial, e.g. image of the testing element, weather of the day, settings, etc.
  • Remote collaborations can improve the working efficiency.
  • Auto-grouping of data is a very welcoming add-on.

Ideation

Competitive Analysis

We didn't find any direct competitors of the Workspace, meaning competitors who serve the same group of user with similar functionalities. But we did find a few indirect competitors who have similar philosophy as the Workspace. And they are iCloud, Google Suite, Office 365, Adobe Creative Cloud, Autodesk A360.

The analysis was focused on the landing page, and the information architecture design.

Sketches

Some lo-fi wireframes were used for demonstrating ideas and gathering feedback.

Design

With the feedback we got from the team, I went through a few rounds of design iteration. And finally confirmed the design.

Design System

As we were aiming to build a platform and allow different products to provide services for their users. Having a design system that guides designers of different product to come up with designs that give a consistent look and feel as the Workspace is crucial. Hence a bit part of the project was focusing on the Design System.

Prototype & Test

Prototype & Usability Testing

Before move to the implementation, I decided to create an interactive prototype with Figma, conduct usability testings with a few users to spot potential usability issues. For usability testing, I conducted remote, moderated Think Aloud testing over Zoom. I tested around the key tasks I identified earlier in the process, asking the user to find a particular measurement and its related information, asking the user to share and export the measurement, asking the user to find probes and their contract information.

Each task was followed by a SEQ rating. To understand the overall feeling, at the end of the test, I added a SUS survey and had a short discussion with the tester.

I summarized my observation by a rainbow chart.

No major issues were found from the usability testing. But a few touch ups were made.

Result

The Workspace was then luanched in mid of 2021. And we are continuing improving it by adding more functionalities and supporting the integration of other products. A quote from our product manager - "Workspace helps a lot while we are selling our products. The accessibility of data and collaboration functionality have become a unique selling point for our products."
