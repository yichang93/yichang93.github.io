Overview
OpenPO aims to help merchants to manage their PO (pre-order) business efficiently. As a UX designer, I am responsible for conducting user research, identifying problems, proposing solutions, and validating solutions. OpenPO is an ongoing project and is still in the validating phase.

User Interviews

I kicked off the project by conducting user interviews. Together with the local Indonesian team, we recruited 15 merchants who are doing PO business in the field of F&B. Through the interviews, I found that most interviewees treat PO business as a side business, meaning they have a fulltime job apart from the PO business.

"I'm a single mom. I am doing PO get to get some extra money. By doing pre-order I can control the number of orders and when to produce."

Journey Map

This journey map summarized how our interviewees operate their PO business.

Brainstorming

After the initial research, I conducted a brainstorming section with the design team to define the problem statement and the user flow for our MVP.

The Problems

PO business owners need to provide information that customers need timely, accurately, without spending too much time.

PO business owners need to manage the order status in a single place to make the operation work more efficient.

Low-Fidelity Prototype

With the low-fidelity prototypes, the pre-order management and the general structure of the service could easily be tested in internal usability tests. Without much effort, adjustments could be made before going into the much more costly digital implementation.

Visual Style

The visual design was developed by iterating from mood boards and styletiles to the UI kit and finally to creating a first version of the style guide.

Validation

Before implementing the design and release to the market, the product management team wanted to validate if the proposed solution is what our potential customers want.

Proposed Validation Plan
  1. Create a pre-launch page for the product
  2. Spread the website to PO business owners on Instagram
  3. Track the percentage of people want to get early access to the project of people we reached out
  4. Track the organic traffic of the website

Future Plan

It depends on the result of the validation plan. If we got more than 50% of people to sign up for our first release, we would proceed to implementation. Otherwise, we would conduct in-depth research to understand why people didn't sign up and adjust our plan accordingly.
